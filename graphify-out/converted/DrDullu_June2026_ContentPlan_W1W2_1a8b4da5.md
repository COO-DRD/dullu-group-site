| Most Kenyan businesses don't have a marketing problem.

They have a pipeline problem.

Here's the difference — and why it's costing founders millions every year:

→ A marketing problem means people don't know you exist.
→ A pipeline problem means people find you, show interest, and then... nothing happens.

The pattern is always the same:

A real estate firm in Westlands spends KES 80,000/month on Meta ads.
Their Facebook page has 12,000 followers.
Reels get 4,000 views.

But when I ask: "How many qualified leads did that generate last month?"

Silence.

Not because the marketing failed.
Because there was no pipeline behind it to catch what the marketing created.

No CRM. No follow-up sequence. No qualification system.
Just a social media manager posting and a founder hoping.

Here's what I know after building automation systems for African businesses:

Visibility without pipeline is just expensive noise.

The companies winning right now in Kenya are not the ones with the best content.
They are the ones with the best systems behind the content.

The pipeline is the product.

This week I'll break down exactly what that system looks like.

Follow Dr. Dullu if you're an African founder who builds seriously.

→ Active LinkedIn company page

→ Has been operating 3+ years

The more specific the ICP, the higher the pipeline conversion.
Vague targeting is expensive targeting.

STEP 2 — DATA SOURCING & ENRICHMENT

We pull a list of companies matching the ICP criteria.
For each company:
→ Verify the primary decision-maker (CEO, MD, or Head of Sales)
→ Confirm working email or LinkedIn profile
→ Flag pain signals: Is their site outdated? No contact form? Hiring for sales?

This becomes your scored, ranked prospect list.

STEP 3 — PERSONALISED FIRST CONTACT

Each outreach message references something specific:
→ "I noticed your company has been expanding into [X market]..."
→ "I saw you're currently hiring for a sales role — that usually means..."

This is not a blast. It's a signal that you've done homework.
Response rates: 3–4x higher than generic templates.

Every prospect moves through stages:
Contacted → Opened → Responded → In Conversation → Proposal Sent → Closed

At no point should a lead exist outside this system.
No "I think I emailed them last week."
No WhatsApp thread that you can't search.
A tracked pipeline is a managed pipeline.

STEP 5 — AUTOMATED FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCES

Day 1: First contact
Day 4: Follow-up if no response — shorter, different angle
Day 9: Final touch — adds value, no pressure
