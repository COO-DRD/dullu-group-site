4unter
Zero-Budget Go-To-Market Plan · Kilifi, Kenya

Prepared: May 2026  ·  Dullu Digital

# 1. The Situation (Honest Assessment)
Budget: KES 0. Location: Kilifi, Kenya. Asset: a working product that solves a real, painful problem for sales people across Kenya. The constraint forces creativity — which is an advantage, not a handicap.
The single biggest insight: 4unter is its own marketing machine. Every person who uses it to close a deal becomes a proof point. The first 10 customers don't need an ad budget — they need a personal, high-trust conversation.

# 2. Who We're Targeting First
Primary — the people with the most acute pain:
- Insurance field agents (APA, CIC, Jubilee, Britam, etc.) — spending 60–70% of their week on cold prospecting. They feel this pain every single day.
- Digital agency owners (1–5 person shops) — selling web design, branding, or automation but stuck doing manual outreach. They understand software tools.
- Sales consultants and freelance BDMs — paid per deal, so every hour of research they save is money in pocket.
Secondary (30-day expansion):
- SME-focused B2B founders — accountants, logistics, legal services offering to other businesses
- SACCO/chama financial advisors prospecting for new members

# 3. The Message That Will Actually Land
Do NOT lead with features. Lead with the emotion:
"You spent 4 hours on Google yesterday and made 3 calls. What if tomorrow you had 200 scored leads waiting before you opened your laptop?"
The core fear we solve: being outsold by someone faster. The relief we deliver: pipeline that fills itself. The proof: live demos — real leads, real Kenya, real verticals.

# 4. Channel Strategy — Zero Budget
## 4.1 WhatsApp (Highest Conversion)
WhatsApp is the highest-trust, highest-conversion channel for B2B sales in Kenya. Do not use broadcast lists initially — use personal 1:1 messages only.
- Identify 30 target users in Kilifi, Mombasa, and Nairobi personally — insurance agents, sales consultants, digital agency owners you know or can connect to through 2 degrees
- Message each with a specific, personalised opening: "Lenny, I know you sell insurance in Mombasa. Can I show you something that would give you 50 scored dental clinics in your city in under 3 minutes?"
- Goal: 5 demos per week from WhatsApp alone
- Convert each demo call into a case study message — screenshot + result. Reshare in WhatsApp groups
## 4.2 LinkedIn (Building in Public)
Strategy: document the build, share the outcomes, attract inbound. This works in Kenya because there are almost no SaaS founders doing this transparently.
- Post 3× per week: one honest process post, one result/proof post, one opinion on Kenyan B2B sales
- Content themes: 'How I scrape 200 leads in 4 minutes', 'Why Kenyan sales reps lose deals they should win', 'What I learned running 4unter for 30 days'
- Tag local sales trainers, insurance managers, SME communities in relevant posts
- Target: 1,000 followers in 90 days, 50 DM conversations, 10 trials
## 4.3 Facebook Groups (Contribute First, Pitch Later)
Do not pitch in groups. Build credibility first — answer questions, share useful breakdowns, be known as the person who gives before they ask.
- Target groups: Kenyan Entrepreneurs, Nairobi Startups, Kenya Insurance Professionals, Sales Managers Kenya, B2B Kenya
- Week 1–2: Answer 3–5 questions per group with real, useful answers. No product mention.
- Week 3+: Share a free mini-guide ('How to generate 50 leads in Nairobi under 2 hours') that naturally leads to 4unter
- Only mention 4unter in context: 'This is exactly the problem I built 4unter to solve — happy to show you a demo'
## 4.4 Kilifi as a Strategic Advantage
Kilifi is not a liability. It's a differentiator. Most Kenyan SaaS founders are Nairobi-centric. The coast is underserved and proud of local builders.
- Join Kilifi business community WhatsApp groups and Facebook pages
- Position 4unter as built BY a coastal founder FOR ALL of Kenya — not another Westlands product
- Target coastal verticals first: hotels, logistics companies, restaurants, fishing & agri-businesses
- 'Kilifi startup closes 4 clients in Mombasa using AI' — that's a story TechCabal will pick up

# 5. The Partnership Play (Zero Cash, Revenue Share)
Identify 2–3 sales trainers or coaches in Kenya who have an existing audience of sales reps.
- Offer: they get 30% commission on every paying customer they refer, forever
- Pitch: 'You already teach people how to sell — give them the tool that does the research for them'
- Targets: LinkedIn sales coaches, Strathmore/USIU entrepreneurship lecturers, insurance company trainers (internal)
- Approach: 1 personal WhatsApp message + 1 free account to see the product themselves

# 6. Press & Earned Media
Three outlets worth targeting:
- TechCabal — pitch angle: 'Kilifi-based AI tool helping Kenyan SMB sales reps find leads 10x faster'. Angle the human story (coastal founder, zero budget, real problem).
- Disrupt Africa — pitch angle: 'Kenyan B2B lead intelligence tool designed for sub-Saharan markets'
- Business Daily Africa / Nation — pitch angle: 'How AI is changing the way Kenyan insurance agents prospect'
What you need to write a pitch:
- One compelling result from a real user (e.g. 'found 200 leads in 3 minutes for a Mombasa dental clinic')
- A short personal story: why Kilifi, why sales, why AI
- One live demo link

# 7. 30-Day Execution Sequence
## Week 1 — Foundations
- Day 1: List 30 target users by name. Find their WhatsApp or LinkedIn.
- Day 2–3: Write 5 personalised demo request messages. Send them.
- Day 4–5: Run your first demo call. Record the result (even a screenshot of the lead output).
- Day 6–7: Post on LinkedIn about what you learned. Share the demo result (with permission).
## Week 2 — Pipeline Building
- Send 10 more personalised outreach messages via WhatsApp
- Join 3 Facebook groups. Answer 2 questions per group — no pitching yet
- Identify 2 potential referral partners (sales trainers/coaches). Draft the partnership message.
- Set up a simple landing page for 'Free 30-minute demo' using Calendly or WhatsApp link
## Week 3 — First Customers
- Goal: 3 paying users or 5 active trialists
- Follow up on every demo. Specific follow-up: 'Did you get a chance to try the leads from Tuesday?'
- Offer first 10 customers a 'founding user' deal: locked-in price for life if they pay in month 1
- Share one result on LinkedIn with specifics: vertical, city, number of leads, time taken
## Week 4 — Double Down on What Worked
- Identify the 1–2 channels that actually generated conversations. Go harder there.
- Send press pitches to TechCabal and Disrupt Africa
- Ask first happy user for a testimonial (WhatsApp voice note is fine — transcribe it)
- Begin posting in Facebook groups with your free guide

# 8. How to Get the First 10 Customers
In order of likelihood to convert:
- 1. Demo → personal follow-up → trial → paid (WhatsApp outreach, personal demos)
- 2. LinkedIn post → inbound DM → demo → trial → paid
- 3. Referral from a sales trainer partner
- 4. Facebook group contribution → DM from interested member
- 5. Press mention → inbound sign-ups
Conversion sequence for each lead: demo → 7-day trial → follow-up on day 3 → follow-up on day 6 → offer founding user pricing on day 7.

# 9. What NOT to Do (Avoid These Mistakes)
- Don't broadcast to 500 strangers in a WhatsApp group — you'll get muted and your reputation takes a hit
- Don't rely on Twitter/X — low Kenyan B2B engagement vs effort
- Don't run Facebook ads — you have zero budget and no conversion data yet
- Don't spend time on a Product Hunt launch yet — your market is not on Product Hunt
- Don't ask for referrals before you have a happy user

# 10. 90-Day Targets
- Month 1: 10 active trials, 3 paying customers
- Month 2: 20 paying customers, 2 referral partners live, first press mention
- Month 3: 50 paying customers, 1 sales trainer as distribution partner, regular LinkedIn inbound
Revenue at 50 customers (conservative mix): KES 125,000–200,000/month depending on plan mix. Reinvest into first paid ad experiment once you have conversion data.
