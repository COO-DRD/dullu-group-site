4. Call Script (Local Phone)
Opening — 10 seconds, be direct
"Hi, this is Ian Dullu calling from Dullu Digital. I help [accounting firms / dental clinics / law firms] in Nairobi automate the repetitive work that's consuming their team's time. Is this [Name / the owner]?"
Bridge — get them talking
"Quick question — what does your team spend the most time on that's repetitive? Things like following up with clients, appointment reminders, generating reports, or chasing for documents?"
Let them answer. Whatever they say, you have a solution. Silence is your friend here.
Pitch — tailor to their answer
"That's exactly what we've been automating for [similar business] here in Nairobi. We build a system that handles all of that automatically — so your team only deals with the exceptions. Most clients save 20 hours a week and see ROI within the first two months."
CTA — close for the call, not the sale
"I'm not going to sell you anything on this call. I just want 15 minutes on Zoom to screen-share exactly what this looks like for a [dental clinic / law firm / accounting firm] like yours. What does your week look like — Tuesday or Wednesday afternoon?"

6. Objection Handling
"We're fine, not interested"
"Totally fair. Quick question before I go — what does your team currently do for [their specific task]? Just curious, takes 30 seconds." — Get them talking. Once they describe a pain, you've re-opened the door.
"We already use [software]"
"Good — [software] is solid. We don't replace tools, we connect them. Most clients have 4–5 tools that don't talk to each other so data is still copied manually. Is that something you deal with?"
"We can't afford it right now"
"The economy has been brutal — that's exactly why most clients came to us. Our entry package is KES 9,500/month — less than one day of a staff salary. It typically saves 3–4 days of work per month. Can I show you the math in 15 minutes?"
"Send me information" (brush-off)
"Of course. Before I do — is your biggest headache right now lead follow-up, client communication, reporting, or something else? So I send the right thing." — Never send cold info without a diagnostic question first.
"How is this different from normal software?"
"Normal software does one task. We build systems that connect your existing tools and handle the decisions between them. So instead of your team copying data and manually following up, the system sees the email, updates the record, and sends the follow-up automatically — and decides when and what to send based on context."
"I need to speak to my partner / director"
"Makes sense. When does that usually happen? I can follow up directly after — or I can put together a one-page summary you can share with them. Which would be more useful?"

9. Key Principles
- Get off the pitch fast — ask a diagnostic question. Once they describe their pain in their own words, they've sold themselves.
- Never send cold information without a diagnostic question first. Email + info = spam. Email + question = conversation.
- Close for the call, not the sale. The only job of outreach is to book 15 minutes.
- One vertical per day — your energy and language will be sharper when you're in one mindset.
- Silence on calls is your friend. Ask a question, then wait. Whoever speaks first loses.
- Start with accounting firms — they're mid-year, squeezed by eTIMS compliance, and have clear ROI conversations.
